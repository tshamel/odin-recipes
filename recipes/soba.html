Cold Soba Noodles with Dipping Sauce

What is this?

Cold soba noodles served with dipping sauce is as simple and healthy a meal as it gets!

Ingredients (1 serving):

  • 1 bundle soba noodles
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 100g Japanese silken tofu
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 tablespoon honey (mixed with 1/2 tablespoon water)
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 1/8 cup soy sauce
  • 1 scallion
  • 2 seaweed

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil at medium high heat.
  2. Add the noodles to the boiling water and spread them around the large pot.
  3. Cook the noodles for 2 1/2 minutes.
  4. Transfer the noodles to a colander and run it under cold water to cool the noodles down.
  5. Bring another pot of water to a boil at medium high heat and add salt.
  6. Cut the tofu into small cubes (while in packaging).
  7. Add the tofu to the boiling water.
  8. Remove the pot from the heat and let the tofu cook for 10 minutes.
  9. Transfer the tofu to the colander and run it under cold water to cool the tofu down before placing on top of the noodles.
  10. Cook the hard boil eggs and place on top of the noodles.
  11. Mix the honey and water to make the honey water.
  12. Mix the honey water, chicken stock and soy sauce to make the dipping sauce.
  13. Cut the scallion and seaweed into small pieces to garnish the soba noodles before serving.
